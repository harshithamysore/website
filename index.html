About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Astronomy at the University of Chile, conducting research under the guidance of Professors Gijs D. Mulders and Simon Casassus, focusing on exoplanet demographics.

I am also an adjunct researcher at the Millennium Institute of Astrophysics .

I hold a Master’s degree in Physics from the University of Bangalore.

Outreach

Outreach Team Member (2022–Present)

Since 2022, I have been an active member of the Outreach Team at the Department of Astronomy, University of Chile / Observatorio Astronómico Nacional. I regularly guide observatory tours for school groups and contribute to public engagement events across Santiago.

NOIRLab Vera Rubin Science Ambassador (2023–Present)

As a Vera Rubin Science Ambassador, I support astronomy education and public engagement through NOIRLab outreach programs . This role focuses on making astronomy accessible and inspiring curiosity about the Chilean skies among the broader public.

India–Chile Astronomy Community Initiative

I am collaborating with the Embassy of India in Chile to establish a quarterly astronomy meet-and-greet aimed at fostering connections between the Indian community and Chile’s astronomy ecosystem.

Research

I have been working on exoplanets throughout my PhD, focusing on the following areas:

Exoplanet Demographics

I study population-level properties of exoplanets around low-mass stars, using TESS data to investigate formation and evolutionary processes.

Project 1: Exploring the Radius Valley Among the Lowest-Mass Stars with TESS

Exoplanet Atmospheres

I studied telluric correction techniques using Molecfit and PCA to analyze the atmosphere of a rocky exoplanet. Poster presented at OPAGA 2023: Link to poster

Exoplanet Detections

I collaborated with Prof. Andrés Jordán and Prof. Rafael Brahm on detecting a warm Neptune using TESS light curves and radial-velocity follow-up.
